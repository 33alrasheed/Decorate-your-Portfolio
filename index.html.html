" PhD student and soon to be a Software Developer"

Famed theoretical physicist Richard Feynman once said to “study hard what interests you the most in the most undisciplined, irreverent and original manner possible.” Not until later in academic career did I truly learn to embrace his advice. Earlier in my career, I misunderstood the university educational system. I was always thinking about how to just survive the course and pass. In doing so, I realized that I was so worried about my GPA that I forgot to learn. However, all this changed during my junior year when I started reading more advanced topics and asking my fluid mechanics professor about nano and micro calculations and why they were so different from macro calculations. After seeing my interest, my professor invited me to join his research team as an observer, which proved to be my first experience with scientific research. This completely changed my attitude as I was fueled by the process of speculation, imagination, and calculation in order to observe a phenomenon. My educational goals shifted from obtaining superficial knowledge to a focus on understanding and application, which revolutionized my learning experience. My newfound enthusiasm eventually motivated me to petition the Senior Advisory Board to allow me to conduct original research for my senior design project.
